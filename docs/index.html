04

What it found

▸ The scaffold is the biggest single effect

Codex (0.92) and Claude Code (0.83) tower over every raw model. Same-class models, but a purpose-built coding harness drives Docker far better than a thin tool-loop — the “a scaffold moves the score” thesis, quantified.

▸ Qwen3.7 Max is the best raw model

At 0.62 it matches the CLIs on the four resource/change scenarios (0.91–0.94) for $0.67 total — outrunning every Western flagship in the field.

▸ The novel scenarios are the great separators

retry-storm and false-alarm crushed the field. Qwen aced everything else but went 0.00 on both; even Codex managed only 0.67 on the abstention test. Knowing when a slow dependency will sink you — and when not to act — is genuinely hard.

▸ A sharp cost / quality inversion

Gemini 3.1 Pro is tied for last (0.26) and by far the priciest — $3.64, roughly half the entire OpenRouter spend, ~5× the others. The cheapest models win; the dearest loses.

05

Read with care

Two diagnosis-0.00 rows are under review. glm-4.5 and gemini-3.1-pro both scored exactly diagnosis 0.00 across all 12 instances while still remediating. Two models pinned at a flat zero looks like a submission-parsing artifact — those models likely aren’t emitting the submit tool call cleanly through the neutral harness, so their root-cause writeups never reach the grader. Treat their scores as a floor pending a transcript check, not a settled capability result.
